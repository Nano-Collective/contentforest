The Nano Collective has relaunched its website. The previous version was soft and colourful. The new one looks and reads like what the collective actually builds.

The headline change is the information architecture. The homepage leads with the three principles the collective holds itself to (Privacy First, Open Source, Local First) rather than burying them. The principles are in that order because that's the order they appear in the [Brand Guidelines](https://docs.nanocollective.org/collective/organisation/brand): privacy-respecting, local-first, open for all. Now the site reflects that.

The flagship projects, [Nanocoder](https://github.com/Nano-Collective/nanocoder) and [Nanotune](https://github.com/Nano-Collective/nanotune), each have a dedicated landing page instead of sharing a single paragraph. The full project list (Nanocoder, Nanotune, get-md, json-up) is on the projects page, alphabetised as the brand guidelines ask.

A few things shipped under the hood that are worth naming:

- **Live community proof.** GitHub, Discord, and Reddit stats on the homepage, plus a feed of the latest discussions. Numbers update in real time rather than being curated snapshots.
- **A growth page** that publishes the same data over time, so the trajectory is visible to anyone who wants to look.
- **A branded asset generator** for consistent social imagery across channels.
- **A public analytics page** so the collective's own site traffic is treated the same way it treats everything else: open and inspectable.
- **A leaner codebase** under the same site, with entrance animations and a tightened layout system.

The visual treatment is deliberate. Black-and-white layouts, sharp edges, monospace detailing, an electric-blue accent, an animated ASCII-art backdrop. The brief was to make the site look like what we actually ship: open-source, local-first, developer-facing. The previous design was pleasant. The new one is honest about what the collective is.

This is also the foundation for what's coming. The site now has the room to announce the tools along the shape we keep talking about, software whose job is to coordinate agents and surface their work to humans, starting with ContentForest and continuing from there. Nothing promised, nothing dated.

The Nano Collective is a community-led group building open-source AI tooling not for profit, but for the community. Read the new site at https://nanocollective.org.
