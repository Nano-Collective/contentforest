We relaunched the Nano Collective website today, and honestly, it feels like the site finally caught up to what we actually build 🔥

The old one was soft, colourful, and pleasant. The new one looks like our READMEs. Black and white, sharp edges, monospace detailing, an electric-blue accent, and an animated ASCII-art backdrop on the homepage that nods to the kind of environment our tools actually run in.

The thing I'm proudest of isn't the visuals though. It's that the three principles we hold ourselves to (Privacy First, Open Source, Local First) now lead the homepage in the exact order they appear in our brand guidelines. That's not decoration. That's the filter for what we build and how we build it, and the previous site buried it.

Nanocoder and Nanotune each got proper landing pages too. Before they were sharing a single paragraph. Now there's room to say what each one actually does, link to the repo and docs, and stay in the same voice as the project's own README.

A few things I'm personally excited about under the hood:

- Live GitHub, Discord, and Reddit stats on the homepage. No more curated screenshots.
- A public growth page so the trajectory is visible to anyone.
- A branded asset generator so our social imagery stops drifting between surfaces.
- A public analytics page. We treat our own site the way we treat our projects: open and inspectable.

The site is also the foundation for what's coming. ContentForest has its own page now, and the broader pattern of software that coordinates agents and surfaces their work to humans is something we'll keep shipping on.

More soon. Have a look: https://nanocollective.org
