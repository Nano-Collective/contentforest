Most open-source maintenance is funded by goodwill. Goodwill is not a funding mechanism. It is a gap that gets filled by something (guilt, obligation, guilt) before it gets filled by something structural. The structural version is rarer because it requires someone to give up control of the money and the governance at the same time, and most organisations that hold money are not interested in holding less of it.

The Nano Collective's sponsorship program goes through Open Source Collective. Every dollar that comes in is visible on the public ledger in real time. There are no hidden cuts, no management fees, and no profit-taking. The three principles (privacy-respecting, local-first, open for all) are not a mission statement. They are the architecture. The structure is the point.

Tiers are productised. Individual ($5, $25, or $100/month), Supporter ($250/month), Builder ($1,000/month), or bespoke for custom arrangements. Details at the link below.

The four shipped projects (Nanocoder, Nanotune, get-md, and json-up) are open because the structure demands it. No investors. No profit motive. Everything visible.

The sponsor wall is empty at launch. That is the structure working correctly. The system does not require the money to exist. When it arrives, it goes where it is supposed to go. Early sponsors fund what exists, not what gets built next.
