We changed the headline description of Nanocoder. Not because the tool changed (the principles are the same), but because the old framing was doing the product a disservice.

Previously: "A local-first CLI coding agent."

That reads as local-only, even though the tool has always supported cloud models via OpenRouter, Anthropic, Google, and any OpenAI-compatible API. We were underselling half of what it actually is.

The new framing: "A coding agent in your terminal, running on any model you choose (local models via Ollama, or any OpenAI-compatible API)."

Local-first is still a core commitment. We're actively investing in it, including through separate work on Nanotune, which is pushing small local models to be genuinely good at coding tasks. But local-first is one of three principles the collective holds (privacy-respecting, local-first, open for all), not the only one. The old copy elevated one principle and dropped the other two.

The deeper reason for the shift: the real differentiator for Nanocoder isn't that it's local. It's that it's built by a community collective, not a company.

No paid tiers. No telemetry shipping your prompts elsewhere. No investor-driven roadmap steering what gets built toward what monetises best. No lock-in to one vendor's model. The people building it are the people using it, and the direction is shaped by contributors, not executives.

That ownership model is why Nanocoder can genuinely support any model, because a community collective has no commercial incentive to lock you to a specific vendor. Multi-provider support isn't a feature; it's a consequence of the structure.

Local-first is a direction we're committed to and investing in. Cloud support is how we stay useful while the local ecosystem catches up, and helping it catch up is part of the mission.

Built by the Nano Collective. A community collective building AI tooling not for profit, but for the community.
