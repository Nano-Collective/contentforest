Why write it yourself when your agents can handle it?

That's the question we've been working through at the Nano Collective — and ContentForest is the answer we've shipped.

Here's how it works. A GitHub Action scans our product repos every day (Nanocoder, Nanotune, get-md, json-up). It spins up a two-agent Nanocoder pipeline — one agent drafts the release announcement across LinkedIn, X, GitHub, Reddit; a second agent produces the deeper articles. Both validate their own output. A human opens the app the next morning, reviews what the agents produced, and ships it.

No more per-product manual workflows. No more rewriting the same post in six different brand voices. Just: software orchestrates the agents, humans keep the judgement.

This is the pattern we're betting on. ContentForest is the first tool we've built around it, but it won't be the last.

We're also thinking hard about what it would look like to open-source this — so other teams can run it against their own repos with their own voice and channels. Nothing promised, but it's on the table.

Big credit to the team for getting this shipped. Brijesh K R, Deniz Okcu, Nassim Amar, Matthew Spence, Alexandru Spînu — this one was a team effort.
