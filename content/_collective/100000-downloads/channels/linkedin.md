The Nano Collective just crossed 100,000 total downloads across its open-source projects: Nanocoder, Nanotune, get-md, and json-up.

This milestone is not a vanity number. It is a real signal that a model built on local-first, privacy-respecting, open tooling can find a working audience without investors, without an extraction layer, and without a cloud dependency bolted to every keystroke. Each install is someone choosing that model for their own work.

The Nano Collective is community-led and not-for-profit. Everything it ships is open, transparent, and shaped by the people who actually use it. The Economics Charter locks in how the funds flow. The Open Collective ledger shows every payment in real time. None of that is incidental; it is the operating system the work runs on.

100,000 downloads belongs to everyone who installed a package, opened an issue, reviewed a PR, wrote a doc, or pointed a colleague at one of these tools when they needed it. Thank you.
