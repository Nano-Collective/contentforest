So, Mario Zechner sold Pi.

That is fine, by the way, and he has been honest about it. Pi started as a solo MIT project, it grew into something real, and now it has backing from Accel and Balderton via Earendil. He is upfront that commercial offerings are coming. More transparency than most.

But here is why I think the decision matters, and why I feel good about where Nanocoder sits in comparison.

Pi is now a venture-backed product. At some point, investors need a return, and the question of what serves users and what serves the cap table stops being the same thing. That is not an attack on the Pi team, it is just the structure.

Nanocoder is built by the Nano Collective. Not-for-profit, community-driven. No VC, no holding company, no exit pressure, no cap table. The roadmap lives in the open. Who uses the tool gets a real say in where it goes.

The practical difference shows up in the tool itself: Pi ships a deliberately minimal core and asks you to assemble the rest. Nanocoder ships the rest because we think a local-first coding agent should be useful the moment you install it. Ollama as a first-class provider, an interactive TUI, four built-in modes toggled with Shift+Tab. No paid tier. No telemetry you cannot see. A community collective that cannot be quietly acquired and shut down.

If that matters to you, Nanocoder is built for exactly this moment.
