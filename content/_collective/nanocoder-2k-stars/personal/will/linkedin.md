A year ago Nanocoder was just an idea and a tiny Discord. This week we crossed 2,000 GitHub stars. 🔥

I keep coming back to that number because it doesn't actually belong to me. It belongs to the people who showed up: the ones who opened a PR at 11pm, the ones who filed the bug we hadn't noticed yet, the ones who answered a question in Discord for a stranger. That is the whole engine of this.

We also crossed 10,000 downloads a month, which still catches me off guard. We are a small, volunteer-led group. Nobody on the core team is doing this full-time. We are doing it because we think AI tooling should be open, local-first, and shaped by the people who use it, not by the people who want to rent it back to you.

Massive credit to the Nano Collective core team and to every contributor who has made Nanocoder what it is. Brijesh K R, Deniz Okcu, Nassim Amar, Matthew Spence, Alexandru Spînu, and the dozens of others I have not named. You built this with us.

If you have starred the repo, opened an issue, or just used the tool and liked it: thank you. We see you.

More soon. The next year is going to be a lot of fun.
